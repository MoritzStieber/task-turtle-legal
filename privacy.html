§ 2 Scope of Data Processing (Art. 13(1)(c, d) GDPR)

TaskTurtle only processes the data you enter into the app yourself. This includes:

  • Tasks (title, due date, reminder, status, subtasks)
  • Habits (name, icon, frequency, completions)
  • Goals (title, milestones, progress)
  • Timer settings and focus sessions
  • XP and level progress
  • App settings (sound, dark mode)

No personal data such as name, email address, location or device ID is collected.

§ 3 Legal Basis (Art. 6(1) GDPR)

Your data is processed on the basis of Art. 6(1)(b) GDPR (performance of a contract). Storing your entries is necessary in order to provide the core features of the app.

Access to your calendar and notifications is based on Art. 6(1)(a) GDPR (consent). You can revoke these consents at any time in the system settings.

§ 4 Data Storage and iCloud Sync

Your data is stored primarily locally on your device using Apple's SwiftData framework. No data is transmitted to servers operated by the developer.

If you have iCloud enabled on your device, the data is additionally stored in your private iCloud area in encrypted form via Apple CloudKit. In this case, Apple acts as the processor; the data is not accessible to the developer. Apple processes the data in accordance with Apple's Privacy Policy. You can disable iCloud sync at any time in your iOS settings under [Your Name] → iCloud → Apps Using iCloud.

When you uninstall the app, all locally stored data is permanently deleted. Data synced to iCloud remains until you remove it manually via the iOS settings.

§ 5 Calendar Access (EventKit)

TaskTurtle lets you add tasks and habits as events to your system calendar. The following applies:

  • Access to your calendar only takes place after your explicit consent.
  • The app only writes the data you select to the calendar (title and date). No calendar entries are read.
  • Processing takes place entirely on your device using Apple's EventKit framework.
  • You can revoke calendar permission at any time in your system settings.

§ 6 Notifications

TaskTurtle may send local notifications (e.g. reminders for tasks, habits or timer completion). These are created and processed exclusively on your device. No push notification service via external servers is used.

Permission for notifications is obtained in accordance with Art. 6(1)(a) GDPR (consent). You can revoke this at any time in your system settings.

§ 7 In-App Purchases and Subscriptions

TaskTurtle offers optional premium features via a subscription. Purchases are handled through Apple's App Store and the service RevenueCat. The following applies:

  • Payment processing is handled entirely by Apple. The developer has no access to your payment details.
  • RevenueCat processes an anonymous app user ID to manage your subscription status. No personal data is transmitted to RevenueCat.
  • RevenueCat acts as a processor within the meaning of Art. 28 GDPR. RevenueCat's privacy policy applies (revenuecat.com/privacy).
  • You can manage or cancel your subscription at any time via your Apple ID settings.

§ 8 Third Parties and Data Sharing (Art. 13(1)(e) GDPR)

TaskTurtle does not use any analytics tools, tracking or advertising. No data is shared with or sold to third parties.

The following third-party services are used:

  • Apple (App Store, EventKit, iCloud / CloudKit) – see § 4, § 5, § 7
  • RevenueCat (subscription management) – see § 7

§ 9 Transfers to Third Countries (Art. 13(1)(f) GDPR)

Subscription management by RevenueCat (based in the United States) and iCloud sync by Apple may involve the transfer of personal data to the United States.

Such transfers are based on the EU-US Data Privacy Framework (adequacy decision of the European Commission dated 10 July 2023) and, where applicable, on the Standard Contractual Clauses pursuant to Art. 46(2)(c) GDPR. Both Apple and RevenueCat are certified under the Data Privacy Framework.

§ 10 Storage Period (Art. 13(2)(a) GDPR)

Your data is stored for as long as you use the app. When you uninstall the app, all locally stored data is permanently deleted. Data synced to iCloud remains until you remove it manually via the iOS settings or disable iCloud sync for TaskTurtle.

The anonymous app user ID stored with RevenueCat is retained for as long as required to manage your subscription status.

§ 11 Your Rights (Art. 15–21 GDPR)

You have the following rights regarding your data:

  • Right of access (Art. 15): Since all data is stored locally on your device or in your private iCloud area, you have full access at any time.
  • Right to rectification (Art. 16): You can edit all entries in the app at any time.
  • Right to erasure (Art. 17): You can delete individual entries in the app or remove all data by uninstalling the app and deleting the data in your iCloud settings.
  • Right to restriction of processing (Art. 18): You can disable calendar access, notifications and iCloud sync at any time in your system settings.
  • Right to data portability (Art. 20): Since no data is stored by the developer, all data is already within your own area of access (device or iCloud).
  • Right to object (Art. 21): You can object to the processing of your data at any time by uninstalling the app.
  • Right to withdraw consent (Art. 7(3)): Consents given (calendar, notifications) can be withdrawn at any time in your iOS settings. The lawfulness of processing carried out up to the time of withdrawal remains unaffected.

To exercise your rights you can contact taskturtlesupport@proton.me at any time.

§ 12 Automated Decision-Making (Art. 13(2)(f) GDPR)

No automated decision-making, including profiling, within the meaning of Art. 22 GDPR takes place.
